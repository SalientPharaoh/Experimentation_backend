Consent to Participate in Research


What women don’t want: Females’ perception of dating profiles and behaviour of Autistic Males

You are invited to participate in a research study conducted by Chinmayee Naik from the Department of Psychology at CHRIST (Deemed to be University), Bengaluru. The results of the study will be used to fulfil the requirements of a research paper that has to be published as a part of the degree of Master of Science in Psychology (Neuropsychology).

Procedure 

The experiment takes 20 – 30 mins to complete. The participants will be briefed on the study's objectives and informed about the confidentiality, consent, and ethical principles followed. Then they will be presented a male dating profile and asked to complete a questionnaire based on it. The questionnaire will include descriptive statements of behaviours that occur on a date and should be completed with the profile of the date in mind, as if that person were displaying the behaviours described. Following this, the participant will be asked if they noticed any diagnostic labels related to autism in the profile. Finally, they would be asked to complete a survey about their knowledge of autism.

Purpose of the Study 

The current study investigates females' perceptions of dating profiles and dating behaviours of males with autism. The purpose of this study is to discover the behavioural patterns of adults with autism that female dates interpret as inappropriate, will be willing to end the relationship for. 

 

Potential Benefits of the Study 

The investigation seeks to address gaps in research on the romantic lives of adults with autism in India. The findings of this study can be used to develop interventions for males with autism by teaching them appropriate dating practices. The role of knowledge and awareness about autism diagnosis is being evaluated to elicit empathy and consideration in the neurotypical population for the social deficits of people with ASD.

Potential Risks

            The study is designed with the American Psychiatric Association's ethical principles, such as beneficence and nonmaleficence, in mind. As a result, it poses no risk to the participants. While reading the questionnaire, which describes inappropriate dating behaviours, individuals may feel some discomfort. But the participant can choose to stop the experiment at any time without facing any adverse consequences. Steps will be taken to ensure the participants’ mental well-being. 

Confidentiality

The information collected from the participants, for this research includes demographic details, personal information as well as responses on the questionnaire and survey. This information will be kept confidential, identifiers like name will be eliminated in the final results. Only the authors of the study will be privy to this information. 

Participation and Withdrawal 

Participation in this study is entirely voluntary and all participants will have the freedom to start and stop the experiment at any point. They will not face any adverse consequence for the same. For further help, the researcher can be contacted using the details mentioned further.
